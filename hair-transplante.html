Hair Transplant

Hair transplant is a surgical technique to save us from either complete baldness or baldness in a certain area. Transplantation of hair has been a part of the medical sciences for decades. The methods have been and are being discovered, adopted and experimented to find its efficiency. Hair transplant is a process to relocate healthy hair follicles into the bald regions. Hair can be transplanted from and to any part of the body. What are the advantages of a hair transplant?

Advantages of a hair transplant are:

  • Enhances our appearance – The aesthetic changeover we get after the hair transplantation surgery is the biggest advantage. It makes us look good and feel good, enhancing our confidence in social as well as business interactions.

  • Builds self-esteem – Hair loss both pre-mature and mature can leave devastating effects on our body.   • Natural look– The growth of new hair post-surgery is generally uniform and remains intact for a long period. It gives us a natural look without any traces of surgery been undergone.

  • Simple procedure – The surgery/operation is conducted under local anaesthesia to refrain to patients from feeling any pain or discomfort. The procedure is very simple but meticulous, it takes few sessions to complete an operation.

  • Growth – The transplanted hair grows for their entire lifecycles. Patients need not have to go for any other hair renewing treatments.

What are the types of hair transplanting methods?

There are several harvesting techniques for conducting hair transplantation surgeries. Proper extraction of the hair follicle is of supreme importance to ensure the viability of the hair transplanting. Hairs grow at an angle, and it is important to remove the transplanted tissues at a corresponding angle. There are two main types of harvesting methods:

  • Strip Harvesting– It is also known as a Follicular Unit Transplantation or FUT. The surgeon harvests a strip of hair from the scalp at the back of the head. After the strip is taken out, the wound or incision is stitched. The strip is separated and sectioned into follicular unit grafts. They remove excess fibres and fatty tissue from the grafts to avoid follicular infections. The individual grafts are then inserted in the bald areas keeping in mind the angles and directions to give it a realistic look. Technicians are involved to take care of the technical aspects. The recovery period is around 2 weeks.

  • Follicular Unit Extraction or FUE – The surgery takes place in one long session or multiple small sessions. It is a more meticulous and technical procedure than strip harvesting. Here, hair follicles consisting of 1 to 4 hairs are extracted from different donor areas maintaining the angling of the wounds to maintain a realistic hair pattern. Technicians are required to insert the hair at the bald regions.

    • The method is better than strip harvesting as it leads to small stitches with a recovery period of fewer than 7 days. Also, it is a very expensive procedure as well as time-consuming due to the intricate workings required. A fox test is conducted to test the patients appropriate for undergoing an FUE surgery. Not everyone can go for this surgery according to their wishes.
